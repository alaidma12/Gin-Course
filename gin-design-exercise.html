Chapter 4 Exercise

Design Your Own Gin

Make two choices, name your gin, and discover which style your instincts lean toward.

i.
Choose your base spirit

Your base spirit is the canvas. It's almost flavourless at 96% ABV — but the grain used still influences the mouthfeel and very subtle character of the finished gin.

Wheat
Smooth and light. The most common base for London Dry gins. Clean, neutral, lets botanicals lead.
Corn
Slightly sweet on the palate. Common in American gins. Adds a soft, rounded quality.
Barley
Malty and earthy. Closer to Genever in character. Adds weight and complexity.
Grape
Smooth and slightly fruity. Popular in Mediterranean gins. Adds a subtle wine-like softness.
