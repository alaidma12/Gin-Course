Wheat
Triticum aestivum
Base Spirit · Grain
Wheat
Soft · Clean · Delicate
The most common base for premium gin. Produces a light, neutral spirit with a smooth, almost creamy mouthfeel. Lets the botanicals speak clearly without competing.
Corn
Zea mays
Base Spirit · Grain
Corn
Sweet · Full · Rounded
Produces a slightly sweeter neutral spirit with more body. Common in American and some contemporary styles. Adds weight without heaviness — the sweetness is subtle but present.
Barley
Hordeum vulgare
Base Spirit · Grain
Barley
Malty · Earthy · Substantial
Brings more character to the base spirit — a subtle maltiness that contributes depth. Used in traditional Genever and some Scottish gins. The most distinctive of the four base grains.
Grape
Vitis vinifera
Base Spirit · Grain
Grape
Fruity · Smooth · Distinctive
A wine-distillate base rather than a grain spirit. Produces a softer, slightly fruity neutral spirit. Used in contemporary and French-style gins — adds a distinctive softness to the mouthfeel.
